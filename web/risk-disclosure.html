03 — Cryptocurrency trading risk

You can lose 100% of your capital. Quickly.

Cryptocurrency markets are among the most volatile asset classes in existence. Acting on any signal — regardless of the provider's IQ score or historical performance — exposes you to risks including but not limited to:

  • Total loss of capital. Spot positions can lose significant value in hours; leveraged positions can be liquidated in minutes.
  • Volatility. Single-asset moves of 20–50% within a 24-hour window are common. Larger moves happen.
  • Leverage multiplies losses. Many signal providers post calls with recommended leverage of 10×, 20×, or higher. At 20× leverage, a 5% adverse price move is a full liquidation.
  • Liquidity risk. Smaller-cap tokens can have thin order books. Your SL may fill at a worse price than advertised (slippage), or not fill at all during extreme moves.
  • Funding rate risk. Perpetual futures charge funding every 8 hours. Extended holds on heavily-long markets can bleed even winning positions.
  • Gap risk. Flash crashes, exchange outages, and cross-exchange arbitrage can cause prices to skip past stop levels without triggering.
  • Systemic risk. Exchange insolvency, stablecoin depegging, smart-contract exploits, network congestion, or regulatory action can wipe out positions regardless of signal quality.
A hard truth

Most retail traders who use leverage lose money over time. Even a provider with a 60% win rate can leave a subscriber unprofitable after fees, slippage, funding, and behavioral error. Our IQ score measures the signal, not what happens when you trade it.

04 — Past performance

Past performance does not predict future performance.

Every data point, score, ranking, chart, and sparkline on AgoraIQ reflects what happened — not what will happen. A provider who ranked #1 last month may rank #30 next month. A signal strategy that worked in a trending market may fail completely in chop.

Providers' relative rankings on our leaderboard change continuously. Our own published example calculations show providers with both positive and negative average R-multiples. We show the tail of underperforming providers as honestly as we show the top. This is intentional: we want you to see that performance ranges widely and changes over time.

Our methodology (documented in full at /methodology.html) uses a rolling 30-day window. If you subscribe to a provider based on a 30-day leaderboard snapshot, nothing guarantees that the next 30 days of their signals will perform anywhere close to the previous 30 days.

05 — Limits of our methodology

Our own methodology has blind spots. We document them.

Our methodology page explicitly lists limitations of the scoring system. We won't repeat that here — read /methodology.html#limits — but in brief:

  • R-multiples are computed on mid-candle pricing, without adjusting for trading fees, funding rates, or execution slippage. Your realized return will be lower than our quoted R.
  • We do not adjust for market regime. Providers who mostly posted longs during an uptrend will score better than balanced providers.
  • We cannot observe private or paid-only signal groups. The leaderboard reflects public-call performance only.
  • Providers can potentially game consistency scores by only posting in favorable conditions. We partially penalize this with a sample-weight cap; it's not a complete fix.
  • Judgment calls in signal resolution (e.g., TP + SL in the same candle) are documented and conservative, but still subject to disagreement.
