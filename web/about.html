About AgoraIQ

We grade signal providers by what actually happens. Not by what they screenshot.

Most crypto signal groups pick their wins for the group chat and quietly delete the losses. AgoraIQ is the layer that captures every call, scores it against live market outcomes, and publishes the math. If a provider has edge, our leaderboard will show it. If they don't, it'll show that too.

What AgoraIQ is

A proof layer, not a signal group.

We don't generate signals. We grade the people who do. Every public crypto signal we can see — on Telegram, Discord, and X — gets ingested the moment it's posted, hashed so it can't be silently altered, tracked against live exchange prices until it resolves, and scored on a composite IQ that blends win rate, risk-adjusted return, consistency, and sample size.

The result is a ranking of signal providers ordered by something that's usually invisible: what their calls actually did. If you've ever subscribed to a Telegram signal group based on cherry-picked screenshots and regretted it, this is the page you wish had existed first.

Full methodology is published at /methodology.html. No black boxes, no proprietary magic — every formula, rejection rule, and judgment call is documented so you can push back on anything that looks wrong.

Why it exists

The signal economy needs a receipts layer.

A typical Telegram signal group operates like this: post every call publicly, screenshot the winners for the sales funnel, delete or quietly forget the losers, and charge subscribers based on what looks like a 90% win rate. It works because there's no neutral record of what was actually called. Each group is a self-contained propaganda channel.

AgoraIQ exists because signals are a data problem, not a trust problem. Once you capture every call at source and hash it, the arguments about who performed stop being arguments. Either the hash chain says the signal was called at 09:14 UTC at entry $67,240, or it doesn't. Either the exchange candle hit TP1, or it didn't. The math either says +1.4R average, or it says −0.3R.

How we operate

Four principles we won't bend.

01

Public methodology

Every formula, weight, and edge-case judgment is documented on the methodology page. Versioned, dated, open to criticism.

02

No editorial curation

We don't hide losing signals, downrank providers we dislike, or upweight friends. If a signal was called publicly, it's on the chain.

03

Tamper-evident chain

Every signal is SHA-256 hashed with the previous record. Modifying a single historical record invalidates everything after it.

04

No custody, no execution

We never hold user funds, never execute trades, never ask for exchange API keys. AgoraIQ is pure intelligence.

What we will never do

Five explicit commitments.
